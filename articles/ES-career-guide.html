Basic Description

An amalgamation of all sciences, social and natural, environmental science is a new, upcoming field with much fewer developments than all the other fields. Concerns sparked from World War 2 and the industrial revolution for the future of our planet have skyrocketed the demand for workers in environmental fields: policy making, habitat preservation, energy conservation, waste management, and sustainable agriculture, the possibilities are infinite.

I’m interested! How do I start?

Typically, most ES-oriented careers start with a bachelor’s degree, but you can always experience the field starting in high school. Clubs that focus on any sort of science-oriented competition, social service such as the Rotary Interact club, or even outside organizations such as the Scouts of America can give insight into environmental-oriented service projects or even learn about the processes of our dynamic world. In undergraduate studies at any university, environmental science degrees are not as popular but are usually combined with various other fields to promote interdisciplinary and a holistic perspectives of thinking.
